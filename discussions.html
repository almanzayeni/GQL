30-Day Anti-Inflammatory Meal Plan for Healthy Aging
This comprehensive guide by EatingWell, crafted by registered dietitians, supports healthy aging through dietary choices that reduce chronic inflammation. It emphasizes nutrient-dense foods like omega-3 rich fish, colorful fruits and vegetables, whole grains, legumes, olive oil, and healthy fats, while avoiding ultra-processed foods and excess sugar. The plan offers weekly structured meals and snacks, with daily calorie targets around 1,800, along with adjustments for 1,500- and 2,000-calorie versions. Protein intake is emphasized to help preserve muscle mass, which naturally declines with age.
7 Ways to Improve Quality of Life for Seniors
This article from Bethesda Health Group highlights the importance of finding purpose in life to enhance overall health. Studies show that seniors with a sense of purpose are less susceptible to cognitive impairment, heart attacks, and strokes, and may even live longer. Engaging in physical activity and learning new things can improve brain health and slow physical decline.

Healthy Eating for Older Adults
Harvard Health explores how physiological changes with aging can affect appetite and nutrition. Reduced calorie needs, diminished taste and smell, and social factors can lead to decreased food intake. The article offers strategies to maintain a healthy diet, emphasizing the importance of nutrient-rich foods and enjoyable eating experiences.
Sleep and Aging: Sleep Tips for Older Adults
This guide explores how aging affects sleep and provides tips to improve sleep quality. Recommendations include creating a restful environment, managing stress, and seeking professional help for persistent sleep problems.
6 Things You Should Do After 5 P.M. to Support Healthy Aging
This article from EatingWell highlights evening habits that can promote healthy aging. Suggestions include sipping decaffeinated herbal teas like chamomile to aid relaxation and sleep, taking evening walks to improve digestion and blood sugar regulation, and establishing a stress-relieving bedtime routine with practices like journaling and meditation. The article also advises staying hydrated throughout the day but limiting fluids an hour before bedtime to prevent nocturnal awakenings, skipping alcohol to avoid impairing sleep and increasing chronic disease risk, and enjoying a balanced, protein-rich, and fiber-filled nighttime snack to maintain glucose levels overnight. Collectively, these habits support better sleep, reduce inflammation, and help prevent chronic illnesses, emphasizing that a consistent and thoughtful evening routine can enhance quality of life as one ages.
